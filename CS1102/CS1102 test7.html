Project Topic

Background of dataset:

  • Company Overview: Geely Auto, a Chinese automobile manufacturer, aims to enter the US market by establishing a local manufacturing unit.
  • Data Collection: The consulting firm has gathered a comprehensive dataset of various car types in the US market.
  • Objective: Enhancing this company competitives with US and European car manufacturers.

Goals:

  • Develop a model to analyze car pricing based on independent variables.
  • Assist management in understanding pricing dynamics to inform car design and business strategies.

We derive our plan into three significant plan

  1. Understanding the data
  2. Asking effective questions
  3. Analyzing data
  4. Representing it in an effective way
Our project explores the key factors influencing car prices using the dataset CarPrice_Assignment.csv. We focused on five critical factors: horsepower, curb weight, engine size, car dimensions, and fuel efficiency. The findings aim to assist sellers, consumers, and developers of predictive models in understanding car pricing dynamics.

We used two chatbots for accuracy (Chat-4o-128k and Claude-3.5-Sonnet-200k) and focused on horsepower, curb weight, engine size, width/length dimensions, and fuel efficiency because these variables were analyzed and emphasized by both chatbots.

Findings

The key findings related to the most influential factors affecting car prices are summarized below:

Variable Correlation with Price Key Insights
Horsepower Strong Positive (≈ 0.81) Higher horsepower is associated with higher prices due to better performance.
Curb Weight Very Strong Positive (≈ 0.87) Heavier cars are more expensive because of their premium materials and safety features.
Engine Size Strong Positive (≈ 0.87) Larger engines could perfrom higher performance, so the prices are higher.
Car Dimensions (Width, Length) Moderate to Strong Positive It is more luxurious and spacious in wider and longer cars, increasing their price.
Fuel Efficiency (City/Highway MPG) Negative (≈ -0.68 to -0.70) Higher mileage cars are generally smaller and less powerful, making them cheaper.

Application Scenarios

The findings from this analysis can be applied in the following scenarios: sellers, consumers and predicitve models.

  • Sellers: Manufactuers and sellers can use these variables to optimize pricing strategies, highlighting performance features for high-priced models plus targeting fuel-efficient models to attract economical consumers.
  • Consumers: Consumers can make use of this analysis to these variables to select cars that fit their budget and requirements, balancing performance and efficiency.
  • Predictive Models: Companies and researchers could do further to build predictive models using the correlation data to estimate car prices under specific conditions.

Discoveries

During this project, we discovered the following:

  • There are strong correlation between engine size, curb weight, and horsepower with car prices. This shows the importance of performance in pricing decisions.
  • Fuel efficiency negatively impacts price, showing a trade-off between efficiency and performance-focused features.
  • If we can use machine learning or regression models, and also with the assistance of AI, these factors can predict car prices with high accuracy.
